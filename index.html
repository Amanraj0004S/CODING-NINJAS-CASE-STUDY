Case Study I
Cheerio AI — From Automation to Intent
Multi-channel Engagement · B2B SaaS · Conversion & Retention
Enter Chamber →
Context

The Problem with "Delivery"

Cheerio AI is a multi-channel engagement platform designed to automate communication across the customer lifecycle. Most businesses don't struggle with acquiring users — they struggle with converting and retaining them at the right moment.

The platform tracks user actions, triggers automated workflows, and sends messages across WhatsApp and Email. It operates on existing demand — not demand creation.

Root Cause Analysis

Five Structural Failures

  • Delivery ≠ Engagement — system optimises for sending, not for user response
  • No User-State Awareness — messages don't adapt to changed intent or post-purchase state
  • Weak Personalisation — rule-based logic instead of behaviour-driven intelligence
  • Linear Workflows — push-based messaging instead of interaction-driven engagement
  • Weak Trust Signals — case studies lack timeframe, baseline, and attribution
Proposed System

Intent-Driven Engagement — Two Layers

Layer 1 — Retention Engine: Updates user state post-purchase, builds intent hypotheses, validates via natural interaction, optimises channel and timing.

Layer 2 — Conversion Engine: Retrieves user context, builds profile via natural interaction, adapts tone and flow, reduces decision friction.

Contextual · Relevant · TrustedAfter

Sanctum — Core Insight

"Automation does not drive growth. Growth comes from systems that understand user intent, context, and timing. The next evolution of Cheerio AI lies in becoming a decision-making system — not just an execution layer."

Case Study II
Coding Ninjas — The Non-Tech Conversion Problem
High-Ticket B2C EdTech · ₹1.5 Lakh Bootcamp · Dynamic Persona Funnel · Checkout Optimisation
Enter Chamber →
Executive Summary

The Business Problem

A significant percentage of highly engaged, non-technical professionals — HR Managers, Sales Leaders, Logistics Heads with 5–12 years experience — drop off at the landing page and checkout stage after completing the initial webinar for a ₹1.5 Lakh Data Analytics Job Bootcamp.

The current funnel is a "One-Size-Fits-All" experience heavily skewed towards 21-year-old CS students. Senior non-tech users suffer from a Contextual Trust Deficit and Imposter Syndrome — fearing the curriculum is too technical and irrelevant to their domain.

North Star Metric: Checkout Intent Rate for Non-Technical Professionals.

Root Cause — The 5 Whys

Why Does She Drop Off?

Persona: A 32-year-old HR Manager. Clicked a high-intent ad. Watched a 45-minute webinar. Bounced without booking.

  • Why #1: She feels Imposter Syndrome — fears the curriculum is built for junior software engineers
  • Why #2: Landing page features generic "Data Analyst" stories at IT companies, highlighting raw technical skills only
  • Why #3: She assumes "Data Analytics" means erasing 10 years of HR experience and starting over
  • Why #4: The funnel never explains "Domain Upskilling" (HR Analytics) vs "Career Switching" (Generic Data Analyst)
  • Root Cause: The entire journey is a static, One-Size-Fits-All funnel — a total Contextual Relevance Failure
User Psychology — JTBD Framework

What Is She Actually Hiring This Bootcamp To Do?

Stated Need: "I need to learn Data Analytics."

Latent Need — The Actual Job-To-Be-Done

"When I am overwhelmed by manual operations and stagnant salary growth in my mid-career, I want to acquire a high-leverage technical skill that integrates with my existing domain knowledge — so I can transition into a strategic, higher-paying leadership role without starting over as a junior."
